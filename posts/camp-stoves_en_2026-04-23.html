Car camping stoves typically weigh 2-8 pounds and run on propane canisters, offering multiple burners and wind resistance. Backpacking stoves weigh under 4 ounces and use alcohol, canister fuel, or solid fuel tablets.

Buying Guide: What Actually Matters

Weight vs. Cooking Power

Backpacking stoves under 4 ounces sacrifice cooking versatility for portability. Car camping stoves over 3 pounds offer restaurant-quality heat control but become impractical for hiking. Match stove weight to your transportation method—car camping allows heavier, more powerful options.

Fuel Type and Availability

Propane canisters cost more but ignite reliably in cold weather. Alcohol burns cleanly but provides lower heat output. Solid fuel tablets work in extreme conditions but leave residue. Consider fuel availability at your destination and backup options for extended trips.

Wind Resistance

Exposed burners struggle in windy conditions, doubling or tripling cooking times. Integrated systems with pots that surround the burner perform better than separate stove-and-cookware combinations. Windscreens help but add weight and complexity.

Ignition System

Piezo (click) ignition eliminates matches and lighters but can fail in wet conditions. Manual ignition requires backup fire sources but offers more reliability. Battery-powered ignition systems drain quickly in cold weather.

Simmer Control

High-output stoves often struggle with low-heat cooking. Look for stoves with flame adjustment ranges if you plan to cook more than just boil water. Alcohol stoves provide minimal heat control—essentially on or off.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size camp stove do I need for my group?

Single-burner stoves work for 1-2 people focusing on simple meals. Two-burner stoves handle groups of 3-6 people or anyone wanting to cook multiple items simultaneously. Calculate roughly 10,000 BTUs per burner for adequate cooking power.

Can I use camping stoves indoors?

Never use camp stoves inside tents, RVs, or enclosed spaces. Carbon monoxide buildup can be fatal. Use camp stoves only in well-ventilated outdoor areas with adequate clearance from flammable materials.

How long do fuel canisters last?

Standard 8-ounce propane canisters provide roughly 2-3 hours of burn time at full heat. Alcohol fuel consumption runs approximately 1 ounce per 10-15 minutes of burn time. Actual consumption varies with heat settings and wind conditions.

Do I need a separate windscreen?

Windscreens improve efficiency in breezy conditions but aren't necessary for sheltered cooking areas. Integrated stove systems with surrounding pots provide natural wind protection. Lightweight foil windscreens add minimal weight for backpacking setups.

What's the difference between threaded and clip-on gas canisters?

Threaded canisters (EN417 standard) create secure connections but require the stove to remain attached during use. Clip-on canisters allow stove removal but provide less stable connections. Most modern backpacking stoves use threaded connections.
